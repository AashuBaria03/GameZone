PUBG

PlayerUnknown's Battlegrounds (PUBG), developed by PUBG Corporation, is an influential battle royale game launched in 2017. Players parachute onto an island, scavenge for weapons, and compete to be the last standing as the play area shrinks. Available on PC, consoles, and mobile, PUBG is known for its realistic mechanics and strategic gameplay.

Esports Format & Rules:

  • Team Size: Squads of 4 players
  • Game Mode: Third-person perspective (TPP) or first-person perspective (FPP)
  • Scoring System: Based on kills and match placement
  • Map Rotation: Erangel, Miramar, Sanhok, Vikendi
  • Game Duration: 30-35 minutes per match

Winning teams are those who survive until the end or have the highest score based on the official points system.

GTA-V

Grand Theft Auto V (GTA V), developed by Rockstar Games, is an acclaimed open-world action-adventure game released in 2013. Known for its expansive world, detailed graphics, and engaging narrative, GTA V offers both single-player and online multiplayer modes.

Esports Format & Rules:

  • Game Mode: GTA Online Multiplayer
  • Team Size: Varies, typically 4-6 players
  • Competitive Modes: Racing, Deathmatch, Heists
  • Objective: Complete missions or races faster than opponents
  • Scoring: Points based on mission completion time, kills, and objectives

Esports tournaments feature different game modes such as Heists and Deathmatch, with rules tailored to the event format.

Spider-Man-2

Marvel's Spider-Man, developed by Insomniac Games and released in 2018, is an open-world action-adventure game set in New York City. Players control Spider-Man, battling villains and completing missions.

Esports Format & Rules:

  • Game Mode: Story mode challenges, Time Trials
  • Objective: Complete missions in the shortest time or highest score
  • Competitive Rules: No usage of special costumes or cheats
  • Scoring: Based on mission performance, time taken, and accuracy

Although not traditionally an esports title, Spider-Man challenges are often featured in gaming events as skill-based competitions.

Valorant

Valorant, developed by Riot Games and released in 2020, is a tactical first-person shooter featuring team-based gameplay and unique character abilities.

Esports Format & Rules:

  • Team Size: 5 players per team
  • Game Mode: Plant/Defuse (Best of 25 rounds)
  • Winning Conditions: First team to reach 13 round wins
  • Agent Restrictions: No duplicate agents allowed per team
  • Map Pool: Split, Bind, Haven, Ascent, Breeze, Icebox
  • Tournament Rules: Standard Valorant esports rules with a map veto system

Valorant's competitive scene follows strict tournament guidelines with a heavy focus on strategy and team coordination.
